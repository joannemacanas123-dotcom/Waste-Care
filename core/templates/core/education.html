How to Segregate and Recycle Your Trash Properly

A comprehensive guide to proper waste management at home

Why Proper Waste Segregation Matters

Proper waste segregation is the first step towards effective waste management and environmental protection. By separating your waste correctly, you help:

  • Reduce landfill waste
  • Increase recycling efficiency
  • Protect the environment
  • Save natural resources

Types of Waste and How to Handle Them

Biodegradable/Organic Waste

Green Bin

What goes here:

  • Food scraps and leftovers
  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells
  • Garden waste (leaves, grass clippings)
  • Paper napkins and tissues
Tip: Consider composting organic waste to create nutrient-rich soil for your garden!

Recyclable Waste

Blue Bin

What goes here:

Paper & Cardboard:
  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Office paper
  • Cardboard boxes
  • Paper bags
Plastic:
  • Plastic bottles (PET)
  • Plastic containers
  • Plastic bags (clean)
  • Plastic packaging
Metal:
  • Aluminum cans
  • Tin cans
  • Metal lids
  • Aluminum foil
Glass:
  • Glass bottles
  • Glass jars
  • Glass containers
Tip: Rinse containers before recycling to prevent contamination!

Residual/Non-Recyclable Waste

Black Bin

What goes here:

  • Dirty or contaminated materials
  • Styrofoam
  • Broken ceramics
  • Diapers and sanitary products
  • Cigarette butts
  • Mixed material items (hard to separate)

Hazardous Waste

Special Handling Required

What goes here:

  • Batteries
  • Electronics (e-waste)
  • Light bulbs and fluorescent tubes
  • Paint and chemicals
  • Medical waste
  • Motor oil
Important: Never mix hazardous waste with regular trash. Schedule a special pickup for proper disposal!

Best Practices for Waste Segregation

Clean Before Recycling

Rinse food containers to prevent contamination

Flatten Cardboard

Save space by flattening boxes

Remove Labels When Possible

Helps improve recycling quality

Keep Bins Covered

Prevent pests and odors
